*   **Start a new adventure**: The ideal option to start from scratch! You will be guided through a process to create your character, choose a world, and define the initial premise of your story.

The main screen is divided into three parts:

*   **Left Panel (Character Sheet)**: Here you can see your character's attributes, skills, and status at all times.
*   **Center Panel (Story and Decisions)**:
    *   **Story Window**: The top part shows the adventure narration.

## The Game System: Rules and Mechanics

RoleMIAster uses a rule system to structure your adventures. Knowing it will help you make better decisions!

### 1. Main Attributes

Your character is defined by 7 attributes representing their innate capabilities. During character creation, these are generated randomly, just like in pencil-and-paper RPGs!

*   **Strength (STR):** Physical power and musculature.
*   **Constitution (CON):** Health and resistance to disease and poison.
*   **Size (SIZ):** Height and weight. A large character may be stronger, but less agile.
*   **Dexterity (DEX):** Agility, speed, coordination, and reaction time.
*   **Intelligence (INT):** Learning capacity, memory, and reasoning.
*   **Power (POW):** Willpower, luck, and affinity with magic or psychic powers.
*   **Charisma (CHA):** Leadership, attractiveness, and persuasion ability.

### 2. Derived Characteristics

From your attributes, other vital characteristics are calculated:

*   **Hit Points (HP):** Your total health. Calculated as `(Constitution + Size) / 2`. If they reach zero, you are in serious trouble.
*   **Magic Points (MP):** The energy used to cast spells. Equal to your `Power` attribute.
*   **Action Points (AP):** Determines your speed and who acts first in combat. Calculated as `(Intelligence + Dexterity) / 2`.

### 3. Skills

Skills represent what your character has learned to do. Measured in percentage (%). The higher, the better.

*   **Base Value**: Each skill has an initial value depending on two of your attributes. For example, `Stealth` is calculated with `Dexterity + Intelligence`. This means an agile and intelligent character will have a natural aptitude for being stealthy.
*   **Improvement**: As you use your skills and complete challenges, you will gain experience and be able to improve them.

### 4. Action Resolution (Dice Rolls)

When you attempt something with an uncertain outcome (like climbing a wall, lying to a guard, or attacking an enemy), the Game Master (AI) will ask for a **dice roll**.

*   **How it works?**: The game simulates a roll of a 100-sided die (d100).
*   **The Goal**: To succeed, the roll result must be **less than or equal** to your value in the corresponding skill.
    *   *Example*: If your `Climb` skill is 45%, you need to roll 45 or less to climb the wall.
*   **Success Levels**:
    *   **Normal Success**: You pass the roll. You achieve your goal.
    *   **Critical Success**: If you roll a very low number (usually less than 1/5 of your skill), you do exceptionally well!
    *   **Failure**: You do not pass the roll. You don't achieve your goal.
    *   **Fumble (Critical Failure)**: If you roll 99 or 100, you not only fail, but something goes terribly wrong.

Don't worry about calculations! The game will show you the roll result and the AI will narrate the consequences.

## Combat System

When facing enemies, the game enters **tactical combat mode**:

### Turn Order (Tick System)
- Combat works by **ticks** (time segments)
- Your action speed depends on your **Action Points (AP)**
- Slower weapons consume more ticks but usually deal more damage

### Combat Options
- **Attack**: Use your drawn weapon
- **Block**: Adopt a defensive stance (consumes stamina)
- **Flee**: Attempt to escape combat
- **Surrender**: Surrender to your enemies
- **Cast Spell** (Fantasy) or **Use Implant** (Cyberpunk)

### Important Rules
- Only **drawn** weapons count for damage
- **To draw**: Click on the **weapon slot** in the equipment panel
- Moving items during combat has penalties
- Items on the **belt** can be used faster

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Combat Preparation**: 
> Drawing your weapon during combat consumes time (**Ticks**) and leaves you vulnerable. Whenever possible, **draw BEFORE** starting a fight!

## Enchantments (Fantasy World)

Sorcerers can **enchant items** to give them magical properties:

### How to Enchant?
1. Learn enchantment spells
2. Select the item to enchant from your inventory
3. Choose the spell in your grimoire
4. The item gains the spell's properties!

### Maintenance
- Enchantments **drain Magic Points** per turn
- If you run out of mana in combat, items automatically disenchant
- You can manually disenchant using the context menu

### Advanced Magic and Spell Usage
*   **Quick Cast (Drag & Drop)**:
    *   **Attack**: Drag a combat spell onto an enemy in the scene.
    *   **Self-cast**: Drag a beneficial spell onto your own portrait (left panel).
    *   **Enchant Item**: Drag an enchantment spell onto an item in your inventory.
*   **Context Menu**: Right-click a spell to see all available options.

---

## Cybernetic Implants (Cyberpunk World)

In the Cyberpunk world, you can enhance your body with **cybernetic implants**:

### Installation
- Visit a **Technosurgeon**
- Buy the implant from their stock
- Pay the installation cost
- The implant is equipped automatically

### Humanity Cost
- Each implant reduces your **Humanity**
- Humanity reflects your connection to your original self
- The more implants, the less human you are

### Uninstallation
- Implants can be uninstalled for a fee
- You can sell them back or keep them in inventory
- Humanity is recovered when removing implants

---

## Service System

In your adventure, you will find **Service NPCs** (merchants, technosurgeons...):

### Trade
- Your **Trade** skill (based on Charisma) affects prices
- You can sell items using the inventory context menu
- Merchants have limited stock that replenishes periodically

### Service Types
- **Merchants**: Sell weapons, armor, and consumables
- **Technosurgeons** (Cyberpunk): Install/uninstall implants
- **Sorcerers** (Fantasy): Sell spells and magic components

---

## Inventory Management

### Load Capacity
- Your character has a **maximum load capacity** based on Strength and Size
- Carrying too much weight penalizes you
- Check the load bar in the equipment panel

### Organization
- **Equipment**: Weapons and armor you are wearing
- **Belt**: Quick access items (useful in combat)
- **Backpack**: General storage

### Drag & Drop
- Drag items between panels to move them
- Right-click to access options (equip, sell, disenchant...)
- **Quick Consumables**: You can use potions and food by dragging them directly onto your character portrait.
